Funny that I'm writing this post 3 days after Valentine's Day. But oh well, I was occupied, couldn't help it.

As a smol kid, I learned about Valentine's Day as a day when chocolate and roses are everywhere. Heart-shaped chocolate, to be specific. And you gift those to the person you love. "This day looks interesting", said young me. Then again, I was probably more into the fact that there was chocolate everywhere, and I liked chocolate.

First year of junior high, I learned about the origin of the day. Supposedly, Saint Valentine of Rome was a martyr for the sake of love itself. Hence Valentine's Day and not Valentine Day. "We really be celebrating the death anniversary of some priest huh", said grade 6 me to my parents. My parents couldn't care less about Valentine's Day either. Back then, I was curious as to why they didn't care but I found it too embarassing to ask. Now, frankly, I'd be surprised if they did. They were married for 30 years at that time already, Valentine's Day has zero shit on that.

Third year of junior high, I fell in love with a girl in my class. And I fell *hard*. Yet, Valentine's Day came and I did nothing for her. The only plan I had in mind was chocolate and roses and I could buy neither. Either that or I was just making excuse for the fact that I didn't care enough to go all the way out. Oh well, glad I didn't confess eh?

Last year of junior high, I learned about Pocky Game and the shenanigans behind the game. Despite the prevalence of the trope in manga and anime, not once did I see a Pocky Game unfolding in real life. Oh yeah, I must also emphasize that I never received a single bit of chocolate during my junior high years. I mean, if someone had some romantic feelings for me, then that would sound kinda nice, but I would also just really appreciate some good chocolate without having to spend money for it. Though, I think I did scrounge some chocolate from some of my friends who received way too much chocolate. So yeah, I didn't complain much.

My three years of high school was as uneventful in the romantic aspect as it could get. I was able to have a crush on someone during junior high. I had a crush on no one during high school. The only thing that ever happened on Valentine's Day was me munching on Pocky sticks with my group of friends because we were all weebs and Pocky was a nice snack. Maybe some fancy ass confessions did happen around my school, but certainly nothing happened in my class. And if nothing happened in my class, then as far as I was concerned, nothing happened.

Then I went to Canada. If I have to pick the worst place for Valentine's Day, Waterloo of Ontario would be it. Do you have any idea just how much snow and ice is there in the entire city around that time? And I haven't even mentioned the horrible ass cold that is always paired with extreme wind, which in case you don't know, can easily make -5°C feel like -20°C on a bad day. And by bad day I mean an average day. And no, I never got myself a girlfriend or even a crush either. Hell, I was still occupied with getting an internship until just recently.

I don't know about you, but I just couldn't be bothered with romance and shit when my mind is constantly occupied with school work and job hunt. Especially job hunt. Good god, what a world we live in. 

Rather, I'd want to do things together with someone whom I consider family and who considers me their family. "So you're saying you want to get married?" Well, spouse just means family + romance, and quite frankly the romance part isn't that important. But yes, it'd be even better if I could cuddle with someone and get spoiled by them.

You know, being able to hug someone with all your love is just really nice.

Then again, I say all of that but I'm not even considered reliable in anyone's eyes. If anything, I'm still one heck of an emotional mess.

I cannot give anything to someone else. And in any healthy relationship, everything must go both ways. 

So yeah, consider this my wishful thinking.

Still, I really wanna hug someone.
